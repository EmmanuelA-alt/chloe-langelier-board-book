PART II — THE BUSINESS
Chapter 7 — Manufacturing

Can she produce these?

All production is executed through an established manufacturing partner in China. This relationship was validated through the successful creation of Chloe's own wedding shoes — a proof-of-concept for both craftsmanship and durability. Following that success, Chloe negotiated improved production pricing and refined detailing.

Banker Note: The manufacturing relationship is not theoretical. It has been stress-tested through a real wedding-shoe production run. The partner offers small-batch flexibility, which is critical during early growth phases.

Production Process

1. Pattern Cutting — Laser-cut for consistency with digital nesting to reduce waste.
2. Stitching & Assembly — Hand-stitched in small batches for line accuracy.
3. Lasting — Controlled heat application around precision lasts.
4. Sole Attachment — Thermal bonding + manual pressing with double-nail reinforcement.
5. Finishing — Individual polishing, cleaning, and inspection for every pair.

Quality Control

Random pairs from each batch undergo flex testing (30,000 cycles), abrasion testing, adhesion pull-testing, and colorfastness testing. Only pairs that pass all criteria move to packaging. Small-batch production allows for tighter quality control than mass-production environments — every pair is inspected, not sampled.

PART III — THE STRATEGY
Chapter 14 — Curated Editions™

How does the business repeat?

The initial rhythm will be approximately one Curated Edition every two months — six curated releases per year. As the company gains confidence, the long-term objective is to move toward monthly Curated Editions.

Why Frequent Drops Create Structural Advantage

Recurring Traffic
Customers return regularly to discover what's new.
Scarcity Pricing Power
Limited quantities support premium pricing.
Faster Data
Sell-through results arrive in weeks, not seasons.
Early Problem Detection
Slower-moving silhouettes identified early.
Banker Note: The Curated Edition™ model directly de-risks the revolving facility. Instead of one large inventory bet per season, the company makes smaller, more frequent production commitments.
