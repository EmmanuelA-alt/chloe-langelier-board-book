Manufacturing & Supply Chain

All production is executed through an established manufacturing partner in China. This relationship was validated through the successful creation of Chloe's own wedding shoes — a proof-of-concept for both craftsmanship and durability. Following that success, Chloe negotiated improved production pricing and refined detailing.

Banker Note: The manufacturing relationship is not theoretical. It has been stress-tested through a real wedding-shoe production run. The partner offers small-batch flexibility, which is critical during early growth phases and directly enables the Curated Edition™ model described in this plan.

Production Process

1. Pattern Cutting — Laser-cut for consistency with digital nesting to reduce waste.
2. Stitching & Assembly — Hand-stitched in small batches for line accuracy.
3. Lasting — Controlled heat application around precision lasts.
4. Sole Attachment — Thermal bonding + manual pressing with double-nail reinforcement.
5. Finishing — Individual polishing, cleaning, and inspection for every pair.

Quality Control

Random pairs from each batch undergo flex testing (30,000 cycles), abrasion testing, adhesion pull-testing, and colorfastness testing. Only pairs that pass all criteria move to packaging. Small-batch production allows for tighter quality control than mass-production environments — every pair is inspected, not sampled.

Most emerging footwear brands compete primarily on aesthetics. Chloe Langelier competes on taste, speed of learning, disciplined distribution, and customer ownership.

The traditional footwear model is slow, seasonal, and often disconnected from real-time consumer feedback. While remaining attentive to seasonal preferences and market conditions, each release is evaluated on its own merits rather than being constrained by a rigid industry calendar. The objective is to improve the quality and relevance of every release. Chloe Langelier does not design collections simply for seasons. The company curates limited-edition releases for moments of customer demand. While respecting the realities of climate and seasonal preferences, product development is driven by continuous customer insight rather than the traditional fashion calendar. Traditional fashion measures time in seasons. Chloe Langelier measures progress in customer learning. Every release is an opportunity to refine the product, strengthen the brand, deepen customer relationships, and create greater anticipation for the next curated edition.

Curated Editions™ — The Release Strategy

Chloe Langelier intends to move away from the traditional four-season footwear calendar. Instead, the company will release Curated Editions™ on a disciplined, recurring cadence.

The initial rhythm will be approximately one Curated Edition every two months — six curated releases per year. As the company gains confidence in manufacturing, logistics, customer response, and working capital turnover, the long-term objective is to move toward monthly Curated Editions, or up to twelve curated releases per year.

Monthly does not mean mass-market or disposable. It means disciplined, curated, limited-edition releases that create recurring customer anticipation. The model borrows the urgency of modern release culture, the discipline of fast-cycle retail, and the restraint of luxury branding. The result is a company that can generate recurring excitement without diluting the premium nature of the brand.

Phased Growth Roadmap

The company will grow in five deliberate phases. Each phase is gated by performance data — not by ambition or calendar pressure. The company only advances to the next phase when the data supports it.

Phase 1 — Year 1

Brand Launch & Market Validation

The company launches with five premium styles released as Curated Edition No. 01. Initial sales are driven primarily through Shopify, supported by Instagram, TikTok, Pinterest, influencer seeding, editorial photography, PR outreach, email capture, and VIP waitlists.

In parallel, the company pursues selective placement in approximately 20 to 30 premium independent boutiques, beginning in Montreal and expanding outward across Canada. Inventory per boutique remains deliberately limited — the purpose is visibility, scarcity, and third-party validation, not volume.

The company would rather place limited quantities in a broader network of carefully selected premium boutiques than oversupply a smaller number of locations. This approach increases brand visibility while preserving scarcity.

Each boutique becomes both a retail partner and a market observatory, providing qualitative customer feedback that complements Shopify's quantitative sales data.

A premium boutique carrying Chloe Langelier sends a clear signal to consumers: this brand belongs in the accessible luxury category. That validation is worth more than any advertising spend.

Phase 2 — Year 1–2

Community Building & Customer Ownership

Shopify is the central hub of the business. Every channel leads back to it: boutique discovery, pop-up events, influencer campaigns, PR, TikTok, Instagram, Pinterest, fashion events, packaging QR codes, email signups, and VIP waitlists.

Wholesale distribution establishes credibility; direct-to-consumer builds profitability.

The company's customer database becomes one of its most valuable long-term assets. It captures email subscribers, SMS subscribers, purchase history, size and style preferences, repeat customers, VIP waitlist members, boutique-origin customers, influencer-origin customers, and edition-specific buying behavior.

This database supports repeat purchases, lower customer acquisition costs, more accurate inventory decisions, and progressively better edition planning. Every interaction makes the next Curated Edition smarter.

Phase 3 — Year 2–3

Regional Expansion

With early sell-through data in hand, the company expands beyond Montreal into Toronto, Vancouver, Ottawa, Calgary, and Quebec City. Expansion decisions are driven by actual customer feedback, boutique performance, Shopify analytics, and product-level sell-through data — not by guesswork.

Direct-to-consumer sales continue to grow as a proportion of total revenue while boutique distribution expands selectively. Existing customers receive early access to new Curated Editions, loyalty offers, referral incentives, and VIP programs. Customer reviews and user-generated content become central to the brand narrative.

Phase 4 — Year 3–4

National Retail Validation

With sufficient proof of demand, the company approaches larger Canadian retailers and premium chains — Simons, Holt Renfrew, Browns Shoes, Hudson's Bay, and Nordstrom Canada where applicable.

The difference is critical: Chloe does not approach these retailers as a speculative new brand with beautiful shoes. She approaches them with data — sell-through rates, customer waitlist size, repeat purchase behavior, boutique performance, Shopify conversion rates, geographic demand, influencer campaign performance, return rates, customer sentiment, and best-performing styles.

The goal is not to say, "We have beautiful shoes." The goal is to say, "We have measurable customer demand."

Phase 5 — Year 4+

International Expansion

Only after Canada is validated does the company consider selective international expansion — United States premium boutiques, Bloomingdale's, Macy's, Nordstrom US, online luxury platforms, selective European boutiques, and Middle East luxury retailers.

Discipline remains paramount. The brand does not chase growth at the expense of exclusivity. Every new market entry is preceded by demand signals, not entered on speculation.

Large footwear competitors often have scale, but scale can create slowness. Chloe Langelier's early advantage is agility: the ability to listen, interpret, adjust, and release new product stories more quickly while maintaining a premium brand position.

Strategic Conclusion

Chloe Langelier Inc. is not simply launching a shoe brand. It is building a premium Canadian luxury footwear company with a loyal direct customer base, disciplined distribution, recurring demand, and an operating model that becomes smarter with every release.

For the bank, this strategy is directly tied to cash-flow reliability. Curated Editions™ reduce overproduction risk. Shopify improves margin and customer ownership. Boutique partnerships provide independent market validation. Operational intelligence improves management visibility and inventory precision. Together, these elements support a disciplined revolving credit model in which inventory is purchased, sold, analyzed, and converted back into working capital for the next release cycle.

Every element of this plan reduces risk:

  • Small batches reduce inventory exposure
  • Frequent Curated Editions shorten feedback cycles
  • Shopify increases margins and owns the customer relationship
  • Boutiques validate demand through third-party credibility
  • Operational intelligence improves management visibility
